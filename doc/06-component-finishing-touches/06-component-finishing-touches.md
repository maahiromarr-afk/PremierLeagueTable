### Reflection

Now that you have created a software component from scratch, it's time to
reflect on that process as well as take some time to think about your
growth as a developer. Below, you will find a series of reflection prompts.

> A common gripe that students express is how much they feel the work they do
> in class fails to map to the real world. Now that you've had a chance to
> complete the portfolio project, how much better (or worse) do you think you
> understand software development and why?

I understand software development better now because I built something step by step instead of just writing small programs. I had to plan first, then design, then code, then test. It showed me how everything connects.


> Also, did the portfolio project surface any gaps in your own knowledge of
> software development. If so, what are those gaps and how did you address them?

I realized I was weak in design-by-contract and using Git correctly. I fixed that by slowing down and following the structure for each part of the project.

> Finally, as a part of completing the portfolio project, to what extent has
> your perspective of software development changed, if at all? In other words,
> is software development something you still enjoy? If not, why not?

My perspective changed. I don’t see coding as just writing code anymore. It’s also about planning, testing, and organizing your work. I still enjoy it, and I feel more confident now.
